Overview

You will be presented articles related to CRISPR/ATC. To complete this job:

  1. Follow the given link and read the article.
  2. Rate the article's quality.

You can use the following criteria, together with your own judgment, to evaluate the articles:

  • Factual Accuracy: Does the article build on scientifically accurate information and solid evidence?
  • Scientific understanding: Does the article provide a correct or a mistaken understanding of scientific observations? Does it interpret correctly their significance and possible limitations?
  • Logic/Reasoning: Do the conclusions follow from the evidence? Are there incoherences in the reasoning?
  • Precision/Clarity: Does the article precisely refer to concepts as they are used by the scientific community? Is there some confusion which might mislead the reader? 
  • Sources Quality: Does the article rely on adequate and credible sources (experts, studies) to back up important claims?
  • Fairness/Objectivity: Does the author depict a complete or biased view of the relevant science? Does s/he hold as correct certain sources without proper justification? Would inclusion of other information change the main conclusion?

Quality Rating

Evaluate the scientific accuracy of facts contained within the text, and the scientific quality of reasoning used.

  • Very High (+2): No inaccuracies, fairly represents the state of scientific knowledge, well argumented and documented, references are provided for key elements. The article provides insights and implications to the reader.
  • High (+1): The article does not contain major scientific inaccuracies and its conclusion follows from the evidence provided.
  • Neutral (0): No major inaccuracies, but no important insight to better explain implications of the science.
  • Low (-1): The article contains significant scientific inaccuracies or misleading statements.
  • Very Low (-2): The article contains major scientific inaccuracies for key facts supporting the author's argumentation and/or omits to mention important information and/or presents logical flaws in using information to reach his or her conclusion.
  • Can't Decide: The article is not in my domain and/or I cannot judge its quality.
